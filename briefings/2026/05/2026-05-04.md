# Daily AI Brief — May 04, 2026
*~5 min read · 18 items · Monday*

> Microsoft Agent 365 went GA on Friday at $15/user/month, opening a fresh SI services lane just as the Pentagon awarded eight rivals classified-network AI contracts and froze Anthropic out — a one-week reshuffle of where enterprise AI dollars will land in Q2.

## 🎯 Opportunity signals
*For founders and service businesses.*

- **Microsoft Agent 365 GA on May 1 created an instant agent-governance services market.**
  *What happened:* Microsoft Agent 365 hit general availability Friday at $15/user/month as a control plane to observe, govern, and secure agents (Microsoft-built, partner-built, or third-party). M365 E7 launched same day at $99/user/month, bundling Copilot + Agent 365.
  *Who can act on it:* Microsoft AI Cloud Partners, M365 SIs, governance/security shops with Intune/Defender experience.
  *Why now:* Microsoft is rolling out the Frontier Engineer Badge via Titan Academy and a 3-day technical bootcamp in May — partners certified now will lead the deployment wave before Intune/Defender public preview lands in June.
  *Angle:* package an "Agent 365 readiness assessment" — agent inventory, policy mapping, MFA-on-agents — and pitch to existing M365 E5 customers as a 4-week paid engagement before the June feature drop.
  [source](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/01/microsoft-agent-365-now-generally-available-expands-capabilities-and-integrations/)

- **OpenAI's Codex SI program is live with seven big-five integrators — sub-contracting opportunity opens now.**
  *What happened:* OpenAI launched Codex Labs and a global SI program with Accenture, Capgemini, CGI, Cognizant, Infosys, PwC, and TCS. OpenAI is embedding specialists inside customer orgs alongside the SI delivery teams. Cognizant and Infosys signed dedicated agreements on Apr 21–22.
  *Who can act on it:* code-modernization shops, regional Codex/Claude Code rollout teams, agent-eval consultancies in MENA, SEA, Pakistan, LatAm.
  *Why now:* the top-tier consultancies cannot staff every region — they will sub-contract regional delivery and legacy-modernization work. Pricing is being set this quarter.
  *Angle:* register as a Codex delivery sub on Cognizant/Infosys partner portals, lead with a "legacy COBOL/Java to modern stack with Codex" 6-week pilot offer.
  [source](https://openai.com/index/scaling-codex-to-enterprises-worldwide/)

- **Pentagon's $200M+ classified-AI contracts shut Anthropic out — defense-tech SIs get a fresh shortlist of 8 vendors to integrate around.**
  *What happened:* DoD on May 1 awarded classified-network AI contracts to AWS, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, OpenAI, SpaceX, Reflection AI, and Oracle. Anthropic is formally excluded over its weapons-and-surveillance use restrictions. Reflection AI is reportedly raising at a $25B valuation.
  *Who can act on it:* US-cleared defense-AI consultancies, IL6/IL7 integration partners, federal-side prompt-engineering and red-team shops.
  *Why now:* Reflection AI is the only frontier-model company on the list without a deep federal SI bench — first-movers can lock in preferred-partner status before Booz/Leidos/Palantir pile in.
  *Angle:* pitch Reflection AI's GTM team as a "FedRAMP/IL6 deployment partner" and offer them a turnkey classified-rollout playbook.
  [source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/01/pentagon-inks-deals-with-nvidia-microsoft-and-aws-to-deploy-ai-on-classified-networks/)

- **Open-source inference is becoming a sovereign-AI procurement category — Featherless's $20M Series A is the bellwether.**
  *What happened:* Featherless.ai raised $20M Series A on Apr 30, co-led by AMD Ventures and Airbus Ventures. It runs 30,000+ open models with sub-5-second hot-swap, flat-rate pricing, and native AMD ROCm. Airbus on the cap table signals defense/aerospace sovereign-AI demand.
  *Who can act on it:* MLOps consultancies, regional inference deployers, Llama/Qwen/DeepSeek fine-tune specialists.
  *Why now:* European, GCC, and South Asian buyers want open-weights inference outside US hyperscalers. Featherless needs partner channels in those regions.
  *Angle:* offer a "private Featherless deployment + custom fine-tune" package to mid-market banks/telcos in Pakistan, GCC, SEA — they want OpenAI alternatives that don't ship data to US clouds.
  [source](https://featherless.ai/blog/series-a)

- **Accenture–Google forward-deployed engineer (FDE) program signals the FDE model is the new enterprise default.**
  *What happened:* The Apr 22 Gemini Enterprise Acceleration Program embeds thousands of joint Google + Accenture FDEs at customer sites for agent prototyping. Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic all have variants now.
  *Who can act on it:* boutiques selling time-and-materials FDE labor, Gemini/Claude/Codex specialists.
  *Why now:* clients are getting used to the FDE model and asking for it from smaller vendors. Productize it before the term commodifies.
  *Angle:* sell "fractional FDE" — 2-day-a-week embedded agent engineer at $X/month — to mid-market clients who can't access the Big-5 SI bench.
  [source](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2026/accenture-and-google-cloud-expand-partnership-to-scale-agentic-transformation-for-global-enterprises-with-gemini-enterprise)

- **EU AI Act Annex III high-risk obligations enforce Aug 2 — Omnibus delay deal collapsed Apr 28.**
  *What happened:* the second EU trilogue on the Digital AI Omnibus (which would push high-risk obligations to Dec 2027) ended without agreement on Apr 28. Unless adopted before Aug 2, original deadlines apply: employment, credit, education, and law-enforcement AI become regulated.
  *Who can act on it:* compliance consultancies, audit shops, EU-facing model-card / fundamental-rights-impact-assessment specialists.
  *Why now:* 12 weeks to Aug 2; SaaS vendors selling into EU need conformity assessments and notified-body review — capacity is already short.
  *Angle:* sell a fixed-price "EU AI Act readiness audit + remediation" — 3-week engagement, deliverable is a binder ready for notified-body review.
  [source](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/)

## 🛠️ New tools gaining traction

- **Craft Agents (by lukilabs)** — Apache 2.0 open-source multi-agent framework launched May 2 with pluggable tool-calling, A2A protocol support, and a runtime that targets <100ms agent-to-agent handoffs.
  *Traction:* trending #2 on GitHub Trending (May 3) within 24 hours of launch; covered by AIToolly and Trendshift.
  *Why it matters:* the first post-LangChain framework purpose-built for the A2A protocol — not retrofitted — and it ships with a permissive license that LangChain still doesn't have.
  [source](https://aitoolly.com/ai-news/article/2026-05-02-craft-agents-oss-lukilabs-launches-new-open-source-agent-framework-under-apache-20-license)

- **Superpowers** — code-agent skill framework that ships a methodology layer (skill files, contracts, eval harness) on top of Claude Code and Codex.
  *Traction:* GitHub Trending top-10 May 2–3; multiple respected AI Twitter accounts highlighting the skill-contract pattern.
  *Why it matters:* solves the biggest practical gap in coding agents — skill drift across long sessions — with a portable spec, not vendor lock-in.
  [source](https://aitoolly.com/ai-news/article/2026-05-03-superpowers-a-new-framework-and-methodology-for-developing-advanced-code-agents)

- **Featherless.ai** — serverless inference for 30,000+ open-weights models with <5s hot-swap and flat-rate pricing.
  *Traction:* $20M Series A from AMD Ventures and Airbus Ventures (Apr 30); fastest-growing inference partner in the Hugging Face ecosystem.
  *Why it matters:* breaks the per-token billing model on open weights — predictable infra cost is the unlock for SaaS pricing on top of OSS models.
  [source](https://tech.eu/2026/04/30/featherlessai-secures-20m-to-expand-serverless-platform-for-open-source-ai/)
