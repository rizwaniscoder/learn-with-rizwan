*For founders and service businesses.*

- **Google just opened a Gemini agent SDK and gave partners a financial reason to build on it.**
  *What happened:* Antigravity 2.0 shipped with a desktop app, a Go CLI, a public SDK and "Managed Agents" in the Gemini API — and Gemini 3.5 Flash beat Claude Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5 on MCP Atlas, Toolathlon, Finance Agent v2 and CharXiv at $1.50 input / $9 output per 1M tokens.
  *Who can act on it:* dev-tooling consultancies, Gemini Enterprise integrators, agent-template shops.
  *Why now:* the SDK is in public preview and Google is openly courting custom-agent builders — first-mover templates published this week will get distributed inside the Antigravity catalog and AI Studio.
  *Angle:* publish 2–3 vertical agent templates (sales-ops, finance close, support triage) on Antigravity SDK in the next 10 days; pitch them as "drop-in Gemini 3.5 Flash starter agents" to mid-market customers waiting on internal Gemini Enterprise rollouts.
  [source](https://developers.googleblog.com/build-with-google-antigravity-our-new-agentic-development-platform/)

- **AWS just bolted $25K extra MDF onto agentic AI partners — on top of the existing $50K.**
  *What happened:* AWS opened three new Agentic AI categories inside the AWS AI Specialization with an additional $25K in Marketing Development Funds for qualifying partners in 2026, and folded agentic builds into the Migration Acceleration Program.
  *Who can act on it:* AWS-aligned services shops, Bedrock implementation partners, Pakistan/MENA cloud consultancies.
  *Why now:* the categories were just opened; certifications and case studies submitted in the next 60 days are how MDF allocations get earned.
  *Angle:* productize a 4-week "Bedrock agent in production" engagement and use the MDF to fund 2–3 lighthouse customers — that becomes the AWS Partner Specialization application.
  [source](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/apn/powering-partner-success-2026-innovations/)

- **The UAE is the fastest-growing AI hiring market on earth — and post-Dubai-AI-Week spend is hitting now.**
  *What happened:* AI-related hiring in the UAE jumped from 32% (2023–24) to 48% (2024–25), the strongest growth rate across all tracked markets; Saudi's PIF-owned Humain is scaling engineering hires for its Riyadh/Dammam data centers; Dubai AI Week (April 6–9, 10,000 delegates) historically triggers a 40–60% hiring spike within 8 weeks.
  *Who can act on it:* Pakistan-based AI services shops, bilingual Arabic/English delivery teams, fractional AI-engineering pods.
  *Why now:* the 8-week post-conference window closes around early June; budgets are committed but FTE pipelines aren't filled.
  *Angle:* cold-outbound to UAE/Saudi heads of AI with a "we ship a working agent in 30 days, with on-shore Arabic-speaking lead, off-shore engineering pod" offer — undercut local consultancies on time-to-value, not rate.
  [source](https://gulfbusiness.com/en/2026/jobs/ai-engineers-wanted-uae-becomes-global-hiring-hotspot/)

- **Sigma's $80M round was led by Databricks, ServiceNow and Workday Ventures — analytics is now an agentic build category.**
  *What happened:* Sigma raised $80M Series E at a $3B valuation, led by Princeville with Databricks Ventures, ServiceNow Ventures and Workday Ventures all participating — explicitly to scale its "agentic analytics" platform. ARR is past $200M with >100% YoY growth.
  *Who can act on it:* BI implementation shops, dbt/Looker migration teams, finance/ops automation consultancies.
  *Why now:* three of the largest enterprise platform vendors just co-signed agentic analytics as a category — their customers will ask for implementation partners they don't have.
  *Angle:* pitch existing dbt/Looker clients a "Sigma agentic analytics pilot" — own the migration, the governance model, and the first 3 agents; charge for the workflow design, not the seats.
  [source](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/18/sigma-computing-seals-80m-funding-round-pivots-toward-agentic-analytics/)

- **Dust hit 3,000 orgs and 300,000 deployed agents — "agent ops" is now a real retainer category.**
  *What happened:* Dust raised $40M Series B (Abstract + Sequoia, with Snowflake Ventures and Datadog in), reporting >90% monthly active adoption, >70% weekly, and zero churn in 2025 across 3,000 organizations.
  *Who can act on it:* boutique agent-build shops, RAG/eval specialists, change-management partners.
  *Why now:* enterprises with 300k deployed agents need governance, eval, and continuous tuning that Dust itself doesn't sell — that gap is the retainer.
  *Angle:* package a "Dust agent-ops retainer" ($8K–$15K/month): monthly evals on the top 20 agents, prompt regression tests, ROI reporting; sell as a Dust ecosystem partner.
  [source](https://tech.eu/2026/05/18/dust-raises-40m-series-b-to-build-the-multiplayer-operating-system-for-enterprise-ai/)

## 🛠️ New tools gaining traction

- **Google Antigravity 2.0** — standalone agentic dev platform: desktop app (Mac/Win/Linux), Go CLI, SDK, and Managed Agents in the Gemini API; supports Gemini 3 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.5 and GPT-OSS.
  *Traction:* shipped at I/O 2026 keynote May 19 with $100 of bonus credits through May 25; public preview free for individuals.
  *Why it matters:* the first credible Gemini-native answer to Cursor/Claude Code, with a partner SDK and enterprise tier from day one.
  [source](https://9to5google.com/2026/05/19/google-antigravity-agentic-developer-suite/)

- **Dust** — "multiplayer" enterprise agent OS for human-agent collaboration, with 100+ data-source integrations and granular permissions.
  *Traction:* $40M Series B (May 18) led by Abstract + Sequoia, Snowflake Ventures and Datadog in; 3,000 orgs, 300k deployed agents, zero churn in 2025.
  *Why it matters:* the European challenger to Glean/Writer for enterprise agent rollouts, and now a Sequoia portco that partners can build a services practice around.
  [source](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/18/multiplayer-ai-startup-dust-swipes-40m-funding-help-enterprises-move-beyond-isolated-ai-assistants/)

- **MCP Apps (SEP-1865)** — official MCP extension that lets servers ship interactive React UIs (dashboards, forms, viz) into Claude, ChatGPT and other MCP hosts.
  *Traction:* formalized as a core MCP spec extension in early 2026; SDKs landed in Rust and Kotlin in May.
  *Why it matters:* turns MCP servers from "tool endpoints" into shippable mini-apps — a new monetization surface for tool builders.
  [source](https://thenewstack.io/model-context-protocol-roadmap-2026/)
