  *Why now:* every Snowflake customer just got a cheaper agent stack with a forcing event — and AWS sellers will need fulfillment partners outside the Tier-1 SIs.

- **AI cybersecurity tooling has eaten appsec in 30 days — and none of it triages itself.**
  *What happened:* Glasswing (Mythos found 10,000+ vulns including a 16-year FFmpeg flaw), OpenAI's Daybreak with GPT-5.5-Cyber (May 11) and Microsoft's MDASH (100+ agent ensemble) all shipped this month. Akamai, Cisco, Cloudflare, CrowdStrike, Fortinet, Oracle, Palo Alto and Zscaler are already plugged into Daybreak. [Glasswing](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing) · [Daybreak](https://openai.com/daybreak/)

  *Why now:* the labs flag the vulns but cannot triage, validate or patch at scale — that labor is the wedge a services shop owns.
  *Angle:* sell a "Glasswing/Daybreak triage and remediation pod" — vetted engineers who take a CVE batch and ship verified patches on fixed SLA. Open-source CMS, codecs and SaaS billing are the soft underbellies.

Free for qualifying missions. [source](https://openai.com/index/strengthening-societal-resilience-with-rosalind-biodefense/) · [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/29/openai-biodefense-program)

  *Why now:* OpenAI just normalized "free frontier access for mission alignment" — the same template will land on climate, disaster response and food security inside 90 days.
  *Angle:* pitch national health authorities in Saudi/UAE/Pakistan a "Rosalind-style sovereign access" wrapper — model access + delivery team + governance — before the consultancies productise it.

## 🛠️ New tools gaining traction

- **Claude Dynamic Workflows** — Anthropic's new orchestration primitive in Claude Code; one session writes an orchestration script that fans out tens-to-hundreds of parallel subagents (capped at 1,000) with iterative verification and resumable state.
  *Traction:* shipped May 28 with Opus 4.8; already the #1 feature in r/ClaudeAI and on AI Twitter, picked up by TechCrunch, The New Stack, VentureBeat and MarkTechPost in 48 hours.
  *Why it matters:* "agent teams" stopped being a config flag and became a first-class primitive — agent-build demo decks need to show this. [source](https://thenewstack.io/claude-opus-48-release/)

- **OpenClaw** — open-source personal AI assistant from PSPDFKit founder Peter Steinberger.
  *Traction:* 302,000+ GitHub stars by mid-May, the fastest-growing repo in GitHub history; integrates 50+ surfaces including WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack and iMessage. [source](https://www.nocobase.com/en/blog/best-open-source-ai-projects-github-2026)
  *Why it matters:* the default substrate for "local agent that controls everything" — relevant if you sell desktop-resident workflows to SMBs.

- **OpenAI Daybreak (GPT-5.5-Cyber)** — vulnerability discovery and patch validation platform built on three GPT-5.5 tiers, with a permissive Cyber model for red teaming.
  *Traction:* launched May 11; Akamai, Cisco, Cloudflare, CrowdStrike, Fortinet, Oracle, Palo Alto and Zscaler are integration partners under Trusted Access for Cyber. [source](https://openai.com/daybreak/)
  *Why it matters:* gives security service firms an OpenAI-blessed path to monetize CVE remediation at scale; natural competitor to Glasswing.
