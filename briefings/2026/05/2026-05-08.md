## 🛠️ New tools gaining traction

- **Hermes Agent** (Nous Research) — open-source self-improving agent built on DSPy + GEPA (ICLR 2026 Oral). Creates skills from experience and reuses them across sessions.
  *Traction:* 135k+ GitHub stars in 10 weeks since the Feb 25 launch — fastest-growing AI agent framework of 2026. GEPA delivers 40% speedup on repeated tasks once an agent has 20+ skills.
  *Why it matters:* the first credible production-ready self-improvement loop. Agents that get better without retraining = cheaper long-running deployments. [repo](https://github.com/nousresearch/hermes-agent)

- **Pit** (Stockholm) — "AI product team as a service": custom production-grade software for internal enterprise operations.
  *Traction:* exited stealth May 7 with $16M led by a16z; participation from Lakestar plus angels from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Deel, Revolut. Already serving multibillion-dollar healthcare, mobility, telecom, and industrial customers.
  *Why it matters:* validates "AI services productized as software" as a fundable category — your competition for mid-market work just got VC-backed. [source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/07/pit-launches-with-16m-led-by-andreessen-horowitz-to-power-ai-native-enterprise-operations/)

- **Visual agent builders dominating GitHub** — Langflow (146k stars), Dify (136k), Flowise (51k) now occupy three of the top five trending AI repos.
  *Traction:* combined ~330k stars; these are the on-ramps non-engineers use to build agents.
  *Why it matters:* if your services pitch is "we'll write Python," you're losing to "we'll configure Langflow." Repackage delivery around these tools or compete with senior-engineer pricing only. [report](https://www.askglitch.com/blog/top-5-trending-ai-github-repos-may-2026)
