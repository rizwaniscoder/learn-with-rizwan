# Daily AI Brief — May 7, 2026
*~5 min read · 19 items · Thursday*

> Anthropic shipped Claude Opus 4.7 plus ten pre-built finance agents and full Microsoft 365 integration on Tuesday — a Wall Street platform play, validated on stage by Jamie Dimon, that turns "Claude implementation" into a packaged services line for every banking integrator.

## 🎯 Opportunity signals
*For founders and service businesses.*

- **Anthropic's ten finance agent templates open a Claude services lane for banking shops.**
  *What happened:* Anthropic released ten pre-built Claude agents covering pitchbooks, earnings analysis, credit memos, underwriting, KYC, month-end close, statement audits and insurance claims. JPMorgan, Goldman, Citi, AIG and Visa are already running Claude in production; financial services is now ~40% of Anthropic's top 50 enterprise customers.
  *Who can act on it:* Claude integration shops, RAG/eval consultancies, MCP server builders, BPOs with finance domain talent.
  *Why now:* the templates ship as starting points, not finished products — every bank still needs configuration, integration with Bloomberg/FactSet/internal data, and eval harnesses. The build window is open right now.
  *Angle:* package a "Claude Finance Agent rollout in 6 weeks" offer — pick one of the ten templates (KYC is the easiest entry), wire it to a mid-tier bank's existing Microsoft 365 stack, charge per agent deployed.
  [source](https://www.anthropic.com/news/finance-agents)

- **CopilotKit's $27M Series A confirms in-app agents are the new chatbot — and they need building.**
  *What happened:* CopilotKit raised $27M led by Glilot, NFX and SignalFire. Its AG-UI protocol (which sits alongside MCP and A2A) sees millions of installs per week; Deutsche Telekom, Docusign, Cisco and S&P Global are production users.
  *Who can act on it:* React/Angular dev shops, product engineering consultancies, SaaS retrofit specialists.
  *Why now:* enterprise SaaS vendors are racing to bolt agents into their UIs before incumbents do. AG-UI is the path of least resistance and most teams have never built with it.
  *Angle:* offer a "from chatbot to in-app agent" sprint to any SaaS startup with a Series A or later — replace their floating chat widget with embedded AG-UI agents that drive their actual workflows.
  [source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/05/copilotkit-raises-27m-to-help-devs-deploy-app-native-ai-agents/)

- **SAP's $1.16B Prior Labs + Dremio double-buy creates an Iceberg/lakehouse skills bottleneck.**
  *What happened:* SAP committed €1B+ to acquire German tabular-AI lab Prior Labs (TabPFN — 3M+ downloads), and is buying Dremio to make SAP Business Data Cloud Apache Iceberg-native. Both deals close Q2/Q3 2026.
  *Who can act on it:* SAP partners, data engineering shops, lakehouse migration specialists.
  *Why now:* every SAP customer will need to re-architect data pipelines onto Iceberg to access the new agentic-AI features. The pool of consultants who can do this is tiny.
  *Angle:* train two engineers on Dremio + Iceberg + SAP BDC this month, then pitch SAP-installed-base mid-market clients in MENA/SEA on "agent-readiness audits" before the migration wave.
  [source](https://news.sap.com/2026/05/sap-to-acquire-dremio-unify-sap-and-non-sap-data-power-agentic-ai/)

- **Sierra at $15B = vertical voice agents are now an enterprise procurement category.**
  *What happened:* Sierra raised $950M led by Tiger and GV at $15B+ post-money, with $150M ARR (up from $100M in November). Reference deployments: Nordstrom (5 weeks), Singtel (10 weeks, 70%+ resolution), Cigna (8 weeks, 80% faster patient auth).
  *Who can act on it:* Pakistan/Bangladesh/Philippines BPOs with bilingual talent, voice-AI build shops, conversational-design studios.
  *Why now:* Sierra's price point and deployment model has set the buyer expectation. Every Sierra deal under $5M ACV is a sub-contract opportunity for regional partners; every deal Sierra loses is one a smaller specialist can win.
  *Angle:* productize a "voice agent in 30 days" with Retell or Vapi for one vertical (insurance claims is the unsexy goldmine) — reference Sierra's resolution rates in the pitch, undercut on price.
  [source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/04/sierra-raises-950m-as-the-race-to-own-enterprise-ai-gets-serious/)

- **Pentagon awarded classified-net AI contracts to seven vendors — Anthropic isn't one. Federal services pipeline is wide open for non-Claude stacks.**
  *What happened:* On May 1, the DoD signed classified-network AI deals with SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, Oracle and Reflection. Anthropic was excluded after refusing "all lawful purposes" terms.
  *Who can act on it:* US-based GovCon AI integrators, FedRAMP-cleared data shops, sub-contractors to the seven primes.
  *Why now:* every prime now has a classified contract with no scaled internal AI services arm — they need delivery partners.
  *Angle:* stand up a small FedRAMP-Moderate practice on top of OpenAI on Azure or Bedrock; pitch sub-prime work to Oracle/Reflection (the two least-staffed of the seven).
  [source](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/01/tech/pentagon-ai-anthropic)

- **BMW i Ventures' $300M physical-AI fund means automotive-grade agents are a fundable services niche.**
  *What happened:* BMW i Ventures closed a $300M Fund III for physical AI, agentic AI, manufacturing and supply chain — Seed to Series B, North America and Europe. Total AUM now $1.1B.
  *Who can act on it:* simulation specialists, ROS/Isaac integrators, supply chain analytics shops.
  *Why now:* corporate VCs only fund startups they can sell to. BMW will write checks specifically to companies that can demonstrate manufacturing-floor or supplier-side deployments.
  *Angle:* if you're building anything for industrial AI, get a 10-slide deck in front of i Ventures this quarter; if you're a services shop, position as the "deployment partner" for their portfolio.
  [source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/29/bmw-i-ventures-has-a-new-300m-fund-and-ai-is-riding-shotgun/)

## 🛠️ New tools gaining traction

- **CopilotKit / AG-UI Protocol** — open frontend stack for embedding agents inside React/Angular apps; AG-UI is the third major agent protocol after MCP and A2A.
  *Traction:* millions of installs per week, $27M Series A May 5 led by Glilot/NFX/SignalFire, supported by Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Oracle, LangChain, Mastra, Agno; Deutsche Telekom, Docusign, Cisco, S&P Global in production.
  *Why it matters:* if MCP standardized how agents access data and A2A standardized how they talk to each other, AG-UI standardizes how they live inside human-facing UIs — the missing third leg.
  [source](https://github.com/copilotkit/copilotkit)

- **Comet Enterprise (Perplexity)** — Perplexity's Comet browser, MDM-deployable, with policy controls and CrowdStrike Falcon integration for enterprise.
  *Traction:* Fortune, AWS, AlixPartners and Bessemer are paying customers; positioned as a direct alternative to Microsoft Copilot and Google Gemini for browser-native enterprise agents.
  *Why it matters:* the first browser-as-agent product with credible enterprise governance — likely the wedge for Perplexity to become an enterprise vendor, not just a search alternative.
  [source](https://www.perplexity.ai/enterprise/comet)

- **TabPFN-2.6 (Prior Labs)** — open tabular foundation model, ranked #1 on TabArena.
  *Traction:* 3M+ downloads pre-acquisition; the technology SAP just bought a company to own.
  *Why it matters:* "ChatGPT for spreadsheets" is no longer a meme — TFMs are now an enterprise-procurement category. Worth learning before SAP locks the best version behind a license.
  [source](https://priorlabs.ai/tabpfn)
