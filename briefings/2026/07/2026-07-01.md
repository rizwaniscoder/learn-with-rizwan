# Daily AI Brief — July 01, 2026
*~5 min read · 18 items · Wednesday*

> Microsoft 365 Copilot Business becomes a permanent SKU at reset prices this morning — every CSP, MSP and AI-services shop now has a fresh reason to knock on 400M+ Microsoft 365 seats, while the EU AI Act high-risk clock ticks down to 32 days and OpenAI's Sol lights up on Cerebras at 750 tokens/sec.

## 🎯 Opportunity signals
*For founders and service businesses.*

- **Copilot for Business pricing reset lands today — every M365 customer is up for review**
  *What happened:* Microsoft 365 Business Standard with Copilot ($23.50/user/mo) and Business Premium with Copilot ($32/user/mo) become permanent SKUs today; standalone M365 Copilot for Business moves from ~$18 to $21/user/mo, and a global M365 price update lands across every purchasing channel. Renewals from now on price at the new rate.
  *Who can act on it:* CSPs, Microsoft partners, AI-adoption consultancies, prompt-and-workflow shops that can run a Copilot ROI audit in a week.
  *Why now:* Every mid-market CFO renewing in July gets a bill shock. That is your foot in the door for a "Copilot value audit → agent build" retainer before Q3 budgets close.
  *Angle:* Cold-outreach template — "Your M365 Copilot renewal quote just went up. We'll show you which seats to keep, which to convert to Agent 365, and what to ship on top by August 1." Ten-day fixed-fee audit, then a monthly retainer.
  [Microsoft Licensing](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/licensing/news/2026-m365-packaging-pricing-updates) · [Cloud Factory breakdown](https://blog.cloudfactorygroup.com/posts/microsoft-365-pricing-packaging-update-effective-july-1-2026-what-every-csp-partner-must-know)

- **EU AI Act high-risk deadline is 32 days out — compliance work is the summer opportunity**
  *What happened:* By August 2, operators of high-risk AI systems (biometrics, credit scoring, employment, critical infra, education, essential services) must finish conformity assessments, technical documentation, CE marking and EU-database registration. The Digital Omnibus "extension" to 2027 is still not law; treat August 2 as live. Non-compliance = up to 7% of global revenue.
  *Who can act on it:* Regulatory-tech consultancies, ex-Big-Four risk practitioners, AI-governance shops, model-eval and red-team teams.
  *Why now:* Enterprises spent H1 waiting on the Omnibus. They're now cornered — 32 days to ship documentation they haven't started.
  *Angle:* Package a fixed-scope "AI Act high-risk readiness sprint" — inventory, risk-tier classification, gap analysis, technical-file drafting, CE-mark path. Sell it in Germany, France, Netherlands, Ireland first. Two-week engagement, €25–75K.
  [Holland & Knight](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/us-companies-face-eu-ai-acts-possible-august-2026-compliance-deadline) · [Latham & Watkins update](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/ai-act-update-eu-resolves-to-change-rules-and-extend-deadlines)

- **Agentic AI's 68-point production gap is now the biggest services line in the market**
  *What happened:* 79% of organizations report agentic AI adoption but only 11% run agents in production, per multiple 2026 enterprise surveys. BCG puts the tech-services opportunity at $200B. KPMG's rollout of Microsoft Agent 365 to 276,000 staff (announced June 9) is the tell — the Big Four are eating everything they can staff.
  *Who can act on it:* Boutique agent-build shops, forward-deployed-engineer teams, orchestration specialists (LangGraph, CrewAI, Claude Skills, Agent 365).
  *Why now:* Frontier Alliance partners (Accenture, BCG, Capgemini, McKinsey) can't hire fast enough. Mid-market wants agents but can't afford Big Four rates.
  *Angle:* Pick one industry vertical (e.g., insurance underwriting, healthcare RCM, legal contract review). Ship a repeatable "agent-in-a-quarter" package with a hosted eval harness. Position as the mid-market alternative to the Frontier Alliance.
  [BCG $200B tech-services report](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2026/the-200-billion-dollar-ai-opportunity-in-tech-services) · [KPMG × Microsoft Agent 365](https://enterprisedna.co/resources/news/kpmg-microsoft-agent-365-enterprise-ai-agents-2026/)

- **Gulf's $12B AI-services spend is still 99% up for grabs for Pakistan/regional shops**
  *What happened:* Gulf countries spend >$12B/year on AI consulting and implementation but Pakistani IT exporters capture <1% of it, per a June 3 ProPakistani/PSEB report. Pakistan's FY26 IT exports crossed $3.4B (+20% YoY) driven by AI integration, cloud migration and cybersecurity work — but the demand outstripping supply is Gulf-side.
  *Who can act on it:* Pakistan, India, Egypt, Jordan and Bangladesh AI shops with English-speaking senior engineers and any GCC on-site presence.
  *Why now:* Saudi Vision 2030 and UAE's AI Ministry are underwriting corporate transformation programs. The gating factor is credibility signals (ISO 42001, AICPA, published case studies) — not talent.
  *Angle:* Two-week push: get ISO 42001 in motion, publish two Gulf case studies (finance or health), then pitch three Riyadh/Dubai targets a month with a fixed-fee "AI readiness diagnostic." Aim at group-level CIOs, not procurement.
  [ProPakistani coverage](https://propakistani.pk/2026/06/03/gulf-ai-market-contributes-just-1-to-pakistans-total-it-exports-report/) · [Arab News on Pakistan IT exports](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2643116/amp)

- **Inference-ops is a category now — Baseten's $1.5B round tells you where the fees are**
  *What happened:* Baseten raised $1.5B Series F on June 22 at up to $13B (2.6× its Series E five months ago). It runs 1B+ inference calls/day across 87 clusters and 18 clouds and grew revenue ~20× YoY.
  *Who can act on it:* MLOps consultancies, latency/cost optimization specialists, GPU-scheduling engineers, model-routing/fallback platforms.
  *Why now:* Every company shipping GenAI features now has an inference bill line item, and the ones with product-market fit are seeing it double every quarter. That's the classic "unglamorous but urgent" services window.
  *Angle:* Fixed-fee "inference audit" — model routing, quantization, prompt caching, spot-GPU fallback. Target Series B/C AI SaaS with $500K–$3M annual inference spend. Baseline savings 25–40%; take a % of savings for the first year.
  [Baseten announcement](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260622645563/en/Baseten-Raises-$1.5-Billion-to-Power-the-Next-Era-of-AI-Inference) · [Startup Fortune analysis](https://startupfortune.com/baseten-raises-15-billion-at-a-13-billion-valuation-as-inference-becomes-ais-most-contested-infrastructure-layer/)

## 🛠️ New tools gaining traction

- **Claude Code (June 27 release)** — Anthropic shipped `/rewind` (resume conversations before `/clear`), improved MCP reliability plus OAuth retries, and cut streaming CPU use ~37%.
  *Traction:* Reuters reported the Claude Code business hit a $2.5B run-rate in Q1 and business subs quadrupled YTD; JetBrains' April 2026 developer survey has Claude Code at 46% "most loved" versus Cursor 19%, Copilot 9%.
  *Why it matters:* Streaming CPU savings alone materially change the unit economics for shops running hundreds of parallel Claude Code sessions on shared boxes. [Anthropic release notes](https://releasebot.io/updates/anthropic)

- **Cerebras Inference (Sol tier, July)** — up to 750 tokens/sec on OpenAI's flagship, per Eric Vishria at Benchmark and OpenAI's own blog.
  *Traction:* First-week signup queue reported "select customers only" as capacity ramps; Cerebras stock (CBRS) closed flat after-hours on the news as the market digested that OpenAI now has a real Nvidia-alternative inference partner.
  *Why it matters:* At 750 tps, agentic loops that used to feel synchronous start feeling like a filesystem call — a step-change in what UIs can do in a live human turn. [OpenAI blog](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/)

- **Modular (now Qualcomm)** — Qualcomm agreed on June 24 to buy Modular for ~$3.9B in stock; Modular's Mojo/MAX stack lets developers write AI code once and run it across CPUs, GPUs and NPUs without rewriting per architecture.
  *Traction:* The acquisition price itself is the signal — Qualcomm is explicitly buying the software layer to break Nvidia's CUDA lock on enterprise inference.
  *Why it matters:* If Mojo becomes the "portable AI runtime" inside Qualcomm silicon, expect Snapdragon-inference startups and on-device agent tooling to spike in H2. [The D[AI]LY BRIEF](https://www.beri.net/article/qualcomm-modular-4b-acquisition-nvidia-cuda-lock-in-enterprise-ai-inference-2026)

## 🧠 New models & tech

- **GPT-5.6 Sol / Terra / Luna (OpenAI, June 26).** Three-tier family; Sol is the flagship (>Claude Opus 4.8 on OpenAI's public evals), Terra is the workhorse, Luna is the cheap-and-fast option. Pricing: Sol $5/$30, Terra $2.50/$15, Luna $1/$6 per 1M tokens. Access still capped at ~20 US-government-approved partners under the June 2 Trump AI executive order. [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/openai-unveils-gpt-5-6-sol-terra-and-luna-models-but-only-accessible-to-limited-preview-partners-for-now-per-us-gov)
- **Zhipu / Z.ai GLM-5.2 (June 16, MIT open weights).** 753B-parameter MoE with 1M-token context and a new "IndexShare" optimization that cuts per-token FLOPs 2.9× at max context. Scores: SWE-bench Pro 62.1 (beats GPT-5.5's 58.6), FrontierSWE 74.4% (near-tied with Claude Opus 4.8's 75.1), MCP-Atlas 77.0. Runs at ~1/6 the inference cost of frontier US models. [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/z-ais-open-weights-glm-5-2-beats-gpt-5-5-on-multiple-long-horizon-coding-benchmarks-for-1-6th-the-cost) · [Semgrep cyber-benchmark](https://semgrep.dev/blog/2026/we-have-mythos-at-home-glm-52-beats-claude-in-our-cyber-benchmarks/)
- **Meta FAIR Brain2Qwerty v2 (June 25).** Three-module deep-learning pipeline that decodes typed sentences directly from magnetoencephalography (MEG) brain signals. Not a product, but the first credible open BCI-to-text baseline for the research community. [Meta FAIR](https://ai.meta.com/open/)
